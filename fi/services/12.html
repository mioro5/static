What is ultrasound therapy?

Ultrasound therapy is a modern physiotherapy method that uses high-frequency sound waves (typically 1-3 MHz) for tissue healing. These sound waves are inaudible to the human ear but create microvibration in tissues, which has a profound healing effect.

Unlike diagnostic ultrasound used for examinations, therapeutic ultrasound is targeted at treating specific areas of the body. It's a safe, non-invasive method widely used in medicine and physiotherapy centers.

Ultrasound therapy can be effective alone or in combination with other therapy forms, offering a natural path to tissue recovery and pain relief.

How does ultrasound therapy work?

Ultrasound therapy works through several mechanisms:

Microvibration

High-frequency sound waves create microscopic vibrations in tissues, stimulating cellular metabolism and improving intercellular connections.

Deep heating

Ultrasound energy converts to heat in tissues, warming tissues from within up to 5 cm deep, improving circulation and relaxing muscles.

Metabolism activation

Vibration increases cell membrane permeability, improving nutrient access and waste product removal from cells.

Anti-inflammatory effect

Ultrasound reduces inflammatory swelling, promotes lymphatic drainage, and accelerates healthy tissue recovery.

Collagen stimulation

Microvibration activates fibroblasts that produce collagen and elastin - essential for skin elasticity and strength.

Pain relief

Stimulates nerve endings and activates the body's natural pain-relieving mechanisms, providing quick relief.

Ultrasound in facial treatments

In facial care, ultrasound therapy is particularly effective:

Collagen production

Stimulates natural collagen synthesis, improving skin elasticity and reducing wrinkle depth

Deep cleansing

Microvibration helps remove dead skin cells and cleanse pores of dirt and sebum buildup

Moisture enhancement

Improves skin's water-oil balance and increases hyaluronic acid production for natural hydration

Texture improvement

Evens skin tone, reduces acne scars, and improves overall skin appearance

Ultrasound therapy process

Assessment

Therapist evaluates the condition and determines optimal treatment parameters

Preparation

Skin cleansing and application of special ultrasound gel

Treatment application

Controlled movements of the device over the treatment area

Monitoring

Therapist monitors response and adjusts intensity if needed

Completion

Gel removal and aftercare recommendations

Important aftercare

After ultrasound therapy, follow recommendations for optimal results:

  • Drink plenty of water - at least 2 liters per day to support metabolism
  • Avoid intense exercise for 2-4 hours after treatment
  • Use sunscreen if treatment was done on facial area
  • Don't touch the treatment area for the first hour after procedure
  • Keep treatment area clean for 24 hours after facial treatment
  • Rest adequately - give your body time to regenerate

Contraindications

Ultrasound therapy is not suitable for everyone. Please inform the therapist if you have:

  • Pregnancy
  • Acute infection or fever
  • Cancer in the treatment area
  • Implanted electronic devices (pacemaker)
  • Deep vein thrombosis
  • Fresh injuries or bone fractures
  • Metal implants in the treatment area
  • Tendency for thrombosis

Frequently asked questions

Is ultrasound therapy safe?
Yes, ultrasound therapy is completely safe when performed by a trained therapist. The energy levels used are carefully regulated and acceptable for the human body. The procedure is painless.
What does ultrasound therapy feel like?
Most people feel mild warmth and pleasant vibration. Some may feel slight tingling, but the procedure should not be painful. If you feel discomfort, inform the therapist immediately.
When will I see results?
Immediate muscle relaxation is usually felt right away. In facial treatments, first improvements may appear within 24-48 hours. Long-term results require 4-8 sessions of regular treatment courses.
How often can I have ultrasound therapy?
Depending on the condition: for acute pain 2-3 times per week, for chronic problems once a week, for facial treatments once a month. The therapist will determine an individual treatment plan.
Can ultrasound therapy be combined with other services?
Yes, ultrasound therapy works excellently with massage, kinesiotaping, trigger point therapy, and other services. Combined approaches often give the best results.
