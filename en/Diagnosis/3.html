What do lateral and medial epicondylitis mean?

These names can be misleading – you don't have to be a tennis player or golfer to experience these ailments. In reality, they're simply the result of everyday overuse.

Lateral Epicondylitis (Tennis Elbow)

Pain on the outer side of the elbow that worsens with wrist extension and gripping. Develops from repetitive movements – computer work, twisting and turning tasks, lifting weights. Pain often radiates to the outer side of the forearm.

Medial Epicondylitis (Golfer's Elbow)

Pain on the inner side of the elbow that worsens with wrist flexion and gripping. Develops from holding and pulling movements – construction work, gardening, carrying weights. Pain may radiate to the inner side of the forearm.

Common Cause

Both develop due to tendon inflammation and micro-tissue damage. Movements that normally don't cause discomfort become painful. The problem doesn't resolve on its own – requires active treatment.

Typical Symptoms

Gradual onset of pain, not sudden injury. Pain intensifies during activity and decreases during rest. Morning stiffness. Grip may be weakened.
