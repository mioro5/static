🔄 What is frozen shoulder?

Frozen shoulder or adhesive capsulitis is a condition where the shoulder joint capsule becomes inflamed and stiff, losing its elasticity. This causes severe pain and mobility restrictions that can last for months to years.

🎯 Main symptoms of frozen shoulder:

  • Severe shoulder pain - especially at night and during movement
  • Limited mobility - raising arm overhead impossible
  • Stiffness - especially in mornings or after stillness
  • Difficulty with daily activities - dressing, combing hair

Frozen shoulder progression - how the disease develops?

📈 The 3 phases of frozen shoulder

Understanding helps choose the right treatment at the right time

1️⃣ Inflammatory phase (2-9 months)

Initial phase: The shoulder joint capsule begins to thicken from inflammation. Severe pain, especially at night. Movement still possible but painful. Treatment is most effective in this phase.

2️⃣ Stiffness phase (4-12 months)

Most difficult: Capsule becomes rigid, adhesions form. Pain may decrease, but mobility is drastically limited. Raising arm overhead impossible.

3️⃣ Recovery phase (5-24 months)

Slow improvement: Mobility gradually returns, but partial limitation may remain. Without treatment, the entire process can take 1-3 years.
