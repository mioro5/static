What is piriformis syndrome?

Piriformis syndrome is the story of one small but powerful muscle that can make your life very complicated when something goes wrong with it.

Piriformis Muscle

This is a deep muscle in the buttock region that connects the sacrum and femur. Its job is to help rotate the leg outward and stabilize the hip. But sometimes it becomes too tense or cramped.

Sciatic Nerve

This is the body's longest nerve, which runs under or through the piriformis muscle and goes toward the leg. When the piriformis muscle becomes rigid, it presses on the nerve - syndrome develops.

Symptoms You Feel

Sharp or burning pain in the center of the buttock, pain radiates to the back of the thigh and toward the knee, pain worsens when sitting, pain may temporarily disappear when walking but returns.

What are the symptoms?

Piriformis syndrome can easily mask itself as other problems, but you have certain signs by which to recognize it.

🎯 Main symptoms:

  • Buttock pain: Sharp or burning pain in the center of the buttock or slightly higher
  • Radiating pain: Pain moves to the back of the thigh, sometimes down to the calf or even to the toes
  • Sitting pain: Prolonged sitting (car, computer) makes pain worse
  • Evening worsening: Pain is stronger in the evening, after a day of movement
  • Numbness: Leg may feel slightly numb or "sleepy"
  • Movement stiffness: In the morning or after long immobility, it's hard to start walking

⚠️ Confusing factors:

  • Sometimes pain is weaker and you think it "went away"
  • Sometimes a short walk helps temporarily
  • Not always on both sides – may be only on one side
  • May resemble back pain, but comes from the buttock
