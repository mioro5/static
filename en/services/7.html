What is cupping massage?

Cupping massage is an innovative therapy method that uses various cups to create controlled vacuum on the skin. This natural and non-invasive technique lifts the skin and underlying tissues, creating an effective restorative effect on the entire body.

The vacuum effect deeply stimulates blood circulation, activates the lymphatic system, and accelerates metabolism, making cupping massage an ideal choice for both beauty care and therapeutic treatment.

Combination of dynamic and stationary techniques

Our cupping massage uses the advantages of both techniques to achieve optimal results:

Dynamic cupping massage

How it works: Cups are actively moved across the skin surface, creating continuous moving vacuum.

Therapeutic benefits:

  • Increases blood circulation over a wide area
  • Effectively stimulates lymph flow
  • Relieves muscle tension
  • Improves joint mobility
  • Reduces swelling and stiffness

Stationary cupping massage

How it works: Cups are placed on specific points and left in place for a set time.

Therapeutic benefits:

  • Deep effect on problem areas
  • Targeted muscle tension release
  • Intensive local blood circulation
  • Joint pain relief
  • Deep tissue relaxation

Why is the combination most therapeutic?

Combining dynamic and stationary techniques allows achieving maximum therapeutic effect:

  • General relief - dynamic technique improves overall circulation and reduces widespread tension
  • Targeted treatment - stationary technique focuses on specific problem areas and pain points
  • Maximum efficiency - the synergy of both techniques enhances muscle tension relief and joint pain treatment
  • Individual approach - therapist can adapt treatment according to your specific health problems

Frequently asked questions

Does cupping massage leave marks on the body?
Cupping massage may leave temporary red marks on the skin that usually disappear within 1-3 days. These marks are normal and show that circulation has been activated. The therapist always adjusts vacuum strength according to your skin sensitivity.
Is cupping massage painful?
Cupping massage should not be painful. You may feel light pulling or pressure, but it should be relaxing rather than uncomfortable. The therapist always monitors your sensation and adjusts vacuum intensity according to your comfort.
How often can cupping massage be done?
For therapeutic effect, optimal frequency is 1-2 times per week. For chronic pain or joint problems, more frequent sessions may be needed initially, then transition to maintenance regime once a week.
When will I feel relief?
Many clients feel muscle tension relief and improved mobility after the first session. For chronic conditions, significant improvement may appear after 4-6 sessions with regular treatment.
What are the contraindications?
Cupping massage is not suitable during pregnancy, recent surgeries, skin diseases, people prone to bleeding or using anticoagulants, heart problems. Always consult with the therapist about your health condition.
